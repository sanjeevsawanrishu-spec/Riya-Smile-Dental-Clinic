What is Digital Dental X-Ray?

Digital dental X-ray, also known as RVG (Radiovisiography), is a modern imaging technology that has revolutionized dental diagnostics. Unlike traditional film-based X-rays, digital X-rays use electronic sensors to capture detailed images of your teeth, roots, jawbone, and surrounding structures.

At Riya Smile Care Centre in Uttam Nagar, Delhi, we use state-of-the-art RVG technology to provide instant, high-resolution dental images. Our advanced digital X-ray system serves patients from Om Vihar, Bindapur, Dwarka Mor, Nawada, and all areas of West Delhi with safe, accurate diagnostic imaging.

Digital X-rays are an essential diagnostic tool that helps your dentist detect problems invisible to the naked eye, including cavities between teeth, bone loss, infections, cysts, tumors, and developmental abnormalities. Early detection through digital imaging can save you time, money, and discomfort in the long run.

Benefits of RVG Digital X-Ray

RVG digital X-ray technology offers significant advantages over traditional film-based radiography. Here's why our patients in Uttam Nagar and West Delhi benefit from our advanced imaging:

  • 90% Less Radiation: Digital sensors require significantly less radiation exposure compared to traditional X-ray films, making it safer for patients of all ages
  • Instant Results: Images appear on the computer screen within seconds—no waiting for film processing
  • Enhanced Image Quality: High-resolution digital images can be enlarged, adjusted for brightness/contrast, and enhanced for better visualization
  • Better Diagnosis: Superior image clarity helps dentists detect problems at earlier stages with greater accuracy
  • Eco-Friendly: No chemical processing required, eliminating hazardous waste associated with traditional X-ray development
  • Easy Storage & Sharing: Digital images are stored electronically and can be easily shared with specialists or insurance companies
  • Patient Education: You can see your own X-rays on screen while your dentist explains findings
  • Comfortable Experience: Small, smooth sensors are more comfortable than traditional film holders

When Do You Need Dental X-Ray?

Your dentist at Riya Smile Care Centre may recommend digital X-rays in various situations. Visit our clinic near Metro Pillar 706 in Uttam Nagar for proper diagnosis if you need:

  • Initial Dental Examination: To establish a baseline of your oral health and detect existing problems
  • Cavity Detection: To find decay between teeth or under existing fillings that cannot be seen during visual examination
  • Root Canal Treatment: To assess root canal anatomy, infection extent, and treatment progress
  • Periodontal Assessment: To evaluate bone levels and detect gum disease-related bone loss
  • Dental Implants: To assess bone density and plan implant placement
  • Wisdom Teeth Evaluation: To check position, development, and potential impaction of wisdom teeth
  • Orthodontic Planning: To evaluate tooth and jaw positions for braces or aligner treatment
  • Trauma Assessment: To check for fractures, displacement, or root damage after dental injury
  • Infection Detection: To identify abscesses, cysts, or other pathologies in the jaw

Our Digital X-Ray Procedure

At Riya Smile Care Centre, our digital X-ray procedure is quick, comfortable, and completely safe. Here's what you can expect:

  1. Preparation: You'll be asked to remove any jewelry, eyeglasses, or metal objects near your face that could interfere with imaging
  2. Protective Measures: A lead apron with thyroid collar is placed over your body to shield you from any scattered radiation
  3. Sensor Placement: A small, comfortable digital sensor is positioned inside your mouth in the area to be imaged
  4. Image Capture: The X-ray unit is positioned, and the image is captured in a fraction of a second
  5. Instant Review: The image appears immediately on our computer screen in high resolution
  6. Diagnosis & Explanation: Your dentist analyzes the images and explains findings to you in detail
  7. Digital Storage: Images are saved in your electronic patient record for future reference

The entire process takes just a few minutes and involves minimal radiation exposure—equivalent to the natural background radiation you receive in a single day.
